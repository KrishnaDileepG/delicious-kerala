Introduction

Welcome to Delicious Recipes from Kerala, your go-to blog for exploring the rich and diverse flavors of Kerala cuisine. From traditional breakfasts to flavorful curries, Kerala’s culinary offerings are a perfect blend of spices, coconut, and fresh local ingredients. In this blog, we’ll take you through some of the most popular dishes of the region, each with its unique story and taste. Whether you’re looking to recreate a beloved dish at home or simply explore new recipes, you’ll find inspiration here for every meal!

Recipes

Appam and Stew

Appam and stew is a traditional dish from Kerala, known for its soft, lacy rice pancakes paired with a mildly spiced coconut-based curry. Appam is made from fermented rice flour and coconut milk, resulting in a pancake with a crispy, lacy edge and a soft, fluffy center. It has a slight sweetness and is often enjoyed with both vegetarian and non-vegetarian stews. The stew is a light, aromatic curry made with coconut milk, flavored with spices like cardamom, cinnamon, and cloves. It can include vegetables or meat, such as chicken or lamb, making it a comforting and flavorful dish. Commonly served for breakfast or dinner, Appam and stew is a staple in Kerala households and is often enjoyed during special occasions and festivals.

Puttu With Kadala Curry

Puttu and Kadala Curry is a classic breakfast dish from Kerala, known for its wholesome and nutritious combination. Puttu is a steamed rice cake made from coarsely ground rice flour and grated coconut, layered and cooked in a cylindrical steamer. It has a soft yet crumbly texture and a mild, slightly sweet taste from the coconut. Kadala Curry, on the other hand, is a flavorful black chickpea curry cooked with onions, tomatoes, and a blend of aromatic spices like turmeric, coriander, and garam masala. The curry is enriched with coconut milk or ground coconut paste, giving it a thick, rich consistency. The combination of Puttu and Kadala Curry is a perfect balance of textures and flavors, making it a beloved and energizing breakfast option in Kerala.

Kerala Fish Curry

Kerala Fish Curry, locally known as Meen Curry, is a flavorful and spicy dish that is an integral part of Kerala cuisine. Made with fresh fish, usually seer fish (neymeen), sardines (mathi), or kingfish (ayakoora), this curry is cooked in a rich and tangy gravy. The key ingredients include shallots, garlic, ginger, tomatoes, and a blend of traditional spices like turmeric, chili powder, and coriander powder. What makes Kerala Fish Curry unique is the use of kudampuli (Malabar tamarind), which adds a distinct sourness to the dish. It is traditionally cooked in an earthen pot (manchatti), enhancing the flavors further. Often paired with steamed rice or Kerala parotta, this curry is a must-try for seafood lovers.
